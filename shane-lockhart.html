Shane Lockhart · Signal & Trace

Former Investigator. Research Systems Builder.

I spent years working inside systems where facts had consequences. Signal & Trace is where I apply that same fact-first mindset to public research, online verification, scam analysis, and AI-assisted workflows.

I no longer work in law enforcement, and this is not an official investigative service. The purpose is to build practical tools and explain disciplined research methods for people who need better ways to separate signal from noise.

Background

Investigative logic moved into a public-facing system.

My background includes criminal investigations, major felony casework, interviews and interrogations, supervisory leadership, corrections administration, tactical operations, and emergency response.

The common thread was disciplined reasoning: define the question, collect the facts, preserve the source, test the contradictions, and make clear decisions without pretending uncertainty is certainty.

Current Focus
  • •Public research and verification workflows
  • •AI-assisted source organization and evidence grading
  • •Scam analysis and suspicious-platform case reviews
  • •Tool testing and simplified research systems for normal users
Case Discipline

Experience under scrutiny shaped the method.

I served as case agent in a capital murder investigation involving extreme violent felony conduct. My role included legally reviewed interviews, case development, and obtaining a confession that withstood court scrutiny. That experience reinforced the importance of rules, source discipline, documentation, and respecting the difference between a lead, an inference, and a fact.

Important Boundary

Signal & Trace does not claim law enforcement authority. It applies investigative thinking to public research, open-source verification, and AI-assisted organization.

Why Signal & Trace Exists

Most people do not need more noise. They need a better structure.

The site is built to help people use public information, search logic, AI tools, and source-grounded workflows more carefully. The tools will keep changing. The method stays focused on clarity, verification, and usable findings.
